Ketchikan

"keh-chuh-kan"

Ketchikan, Alaska, is a picturesque town known as the “Salmon Capital of the World,” nestled along the forested shores of the Inside Passage. Famous for its rich Indigenous heritage, Ketchikan is home to one of the largest collections of standing totem poles, with landmarks like Totem Bight State Historical Park and Saxman Village showcasing Tlingit and Haida art and culture. The town’s historic Creek Street, once a bustling red-light district, is now a boardwalk lined with shops, galleries, and restaurants overlooking Ketchikan Creek. Surrounded by the lush Tongass National Forest, the world’s largest temperate rainforest, Ketchikan offers visitors stunning opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and exploring Alaska’s wild coastal beauty.
